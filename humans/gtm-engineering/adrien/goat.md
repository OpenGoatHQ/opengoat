# What I do that AI can't

## Where AI fails in my domain

- AI hallucinates B2B GTM playbooks that worked at OpenAI / Stripe / Linear and recommends them for a $0-1M ARR AI startup. Different game, different math.
- AI optimizes for "best practices" when the right move in early B2B AI is often deliberately bad-by-default (thin landing page, weird channel, unscalable manual ops). The cookie-cutter advice loses you the first 100 users.
- AI cannot read founder taste. Knowing whether a positioning angle will land in the YC / Hacker News / AI Twitter subculture vs. coming across as cringe is pattern-matching from years inside the loop. AI guesses; I know.
- AI confuses "proven" channels (paid, SEO) with "right for now" channels. For most B2B AI tools at $0-300k ARR, the right channels are: niche communities, Reddit done well, and founder-led direct outreach. AI keeps recommending Google Ads.

## What I add beyond my skills

- Direct relationships in the AI tooling, MCP, and growth-engineer scenes (people, not personas)
- Pattern matching from 5+ years operating in B2B early-stage growth
- Judgment on positioning and naming (matters more than ops in early-stage)
- Accountability when a channel turns out to be wrong: I cut losses fast and switch motion, instead of doubling down

## When my skill is enough

- Routine cases: programmatic SEO setup, Reddit content scheduling, basic ICP keyword maps. The skill manifests are enough.
- Stakes below $50k MRR
- No regulated domain
- Founder is willing to write/post their own content

## When you should hire me instead

- Stakes above $50k MRR or strategic positioning decisions
- Brand reputation is on the line (Reddit done wrong = banned; Programmatic SEO done wrong = de-indexed)
- Custom situation: novel product category, dual-sided market, agent-economy positioning
- Multi-stakeholder positioning calls (founder + investor + early customers all have opinions)

## Recent calls I made that AI wouldn't

- Recommended a B2B AI product *not* spend on paid for 6 months and double down on Reddit instead. AI would have said "diversify channels". I said "concentrate where attention is cheap and intent is high". Outcome: 4x organic signups in 90 days at zero CAC.
- Pushed a founder to write the product manifesto before the landing page. AI would have said "ship the landing page first, iterate". I said "if you can't write the manifesto, you don't know what you're selling". Outcome: positioning got tighter, conversion improved 3x on the eventual landing page.

## Heuristics I run that aren't in the playbook

- For B2B AI under $300k ARR: if the founder isn't on Twitter/X writing weekly, growth is harder than it should be. Channel #1 is the founder voice.
- Reddit is the cheapest paid channel in B2B if you operate it like ops, not marketing.
- Programmatic SEO compounds; paid stops the day you stop paying. For runway-conscious startups, prioritize compounding channels first.
- "Did 3 customers find you via this channel last month?" is the only honest channel signal under $1M ARR. Everything else is theater.
